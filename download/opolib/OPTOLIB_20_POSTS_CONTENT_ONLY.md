**Topic:** Ocular Anatomy Cheat Sheet — Anterior Segment

**SLIDE 1:** Ocular Anatomy Cheat Sheet — Anterior Segment
Save this. You'll need it before every exam.

**SLIDE 2:** CORNEA (5 Layers — from outside in)
1. Epithelium — stratified squamous non-keratinized, regenerates rapidly
2. Bowman's Layer — acellular, modified stroma, does not regenerate
3. Stroma — thickest layer (90% corneal thickness), regular collagen lamellae
4. Descemet's Membrane — basement membrane of endothelium, thickens with age
5. Endothelium — single layer of cells, maintains corneal deturgescence, does not regenerate

**SLIDE 3:** LENS
Biconvex, avascular structure behind the iris
Enclosed in elastic lens capsule
Zonular fibers (suspensory ligaments) hold it in position
Composed of lens epithelium (anterior) and lens fibers (bulk)
Changes shape during accommodation — controlled by ciliary muscle

**SLIDE 4:** IRIS
Colored diaphragm with central aperture (pupil)
Contains two muscles:
• Sphincter pupillae — parasympathetic (constricts pupil)
• Dilator pupillae — sympathetic (dilates pupil)
Divides anterior segment into anterior and posterior chambers

**SLIDE 5:** CILIARY BODY
Located between iris and choroid
Three parts: ciliary muscle, ciliary processes, ciliary epithelium
Functions:
• Accommodation (ciliary muscle contraction)
• Aqueous humor production (ciliary processes)
• Zonule attachment for lens support

**SLIDE 6:** AQUEOUS HUMOR
Clear fluid filling anterior and posterior chambers
Produced by ciliary processes (pars plicata)
Composition similar to plasma but less protein
Drains via trabecular meshwork → Schlemm's canal → aqueous veins
Maintains intraocular pressure (normal: 10–21 mmHg)
